# Draft: Folders Over Agents — The AI Layer Nobody Teaches

**Source:** [Folders Over Agents: The AI Layer Nobody Teaches](https://youtu.be/uYRXYsPS66g) — Jake Van Clief

## Mental Model

Most AI education teaches the **wrong abstraction layer** — agents, frameworks, tool-specific workflows that expire in 90 days. The layer that survives is **context architecture**: how you structure information in folders, how you route between context layers, how you organize thinking in software.

**Tagline:** Folders over agents. Layers over libraries. Methodologies over tools.

## Core Thesis

- AI isn't moving too fast — people are learning the wrong layer
- Transformers (Attention Is All You Need) are 10+ year old patterns; the fundamentals have existed since the 1950s
- Agents, LangChain, AutoGen, etc. change/break with every model update; folder architecture doesn't
- When Claude updated → workflows kept working. When GPT 5.5 dropped → systems got better, didn't break
- **The architecture and context survives. It can abstract infinitely.**

## The 5 Layers of ICM Content

1. **Identity** — Who/what the system is (top layer)
2. **Routing** — How context flows between layers
3. **Stage Contract** — What each stage expects and produces
4. **Reference Material** — Knowledge bases, docs, archives
5. **Working Artifacts** — Active outputs, drafts, builds

## Origin

- Started in 2022 during Jake's Master's research — organizing AI outputs into files for tracking
- Realized the pattern was essentially **git for thinking** — version control for AI context
- Written as an academic paper to bypass gatekeeping (PhDs wouldn't take non-academic work seriously)
- 30,000+ community members, Fortune 500 clients (Pacific Life, KPMG, UK government)
- Real outcomes: 80-95% token/cost reduction, people getting jobs/promotions

## Key Distinction

> "The agent is just the thing using the architecture. When you build a folder system that works, it doesn't care what model you use. It doesn't care what framework or AI you have. It doesn't care what's hot this month."

## Grace Hopper Parallel

She had a working compiler in her hand; experts told her it wouldn't work. Jake draws the direct parallel — having something that works while the establishment dismisses it.
