# Deconstructing Systems

**Source:** Skool: The Foundation / Module 2 / 2.7 From Nazi Psychology to AI Auditing

## Core Idea

You learn more by taking a working system apart than by building one from scratch. Jake deconstructs his own AI auditing pipeline (which runs 1950s psychometric tests against modern LLMs) to show that the real skill is understanding how the pieces connect — APIs, data pipelines, research methods — not just making something work.

## Why It Matters

- Deconstruction reveals hidden assumptions. When you build, you focus on what works. When you take apart, you see what the builder assumed, what they abstracted away, and what could break.
- This is how Jake approaches learning new tools: grab something working, map its layers, then understand each connection point. The system itself becomes the textbook.
- The psychometric tests he uses (F-scale for authoritarianism, moral foundations theory) were designed to study humans in the 1950s. Applying them to AI models reveals something about both the models AND the original tests.

## How I'll Apply It

Pick one working system I already have (like this Framework folder) and write a deconstruction of it: what are the pieces, how do they connect, what assumptions was the builder making? Then use that understanding to improve it.

## Cross-Reference

Skool lesson adds: the AI is 10% of the system. The other 90% is traditional engineering (APIs, async code, parsing, statistics). Deconstruction reveals this — most "AI systems" are mostly non-AI code.
