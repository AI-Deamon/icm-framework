# Raise the Bar: AI in Education

**Source:** Skool: The Archive / Module 2 / 2.2 Raise the Bar (Dec 2023)

## Core Idea

If students can cheat with AI on your assignments, your assignments are too easy. Every major technology (calculator, internet, AI) raised the baseline. The right response is to raise the bar, not build detection tools. The 60/30/10 framework and computational orchestration are the answer to "what do we teach instead?"

## Why It Matters

- The technology stacking pattern: calculators → raised math baseline. Internet → raised research baseline. AI → raising the writing/analysis baseline. Each time: old expert skill → new expected skill → people panic → bar goes up → work gets better.
- The linguistics professor story: if AI handles basic grammar, grammar isn't the skill being tested anymore. Advanced work becomes the starting point. "Generic writing is now visible" — AI users are best at spotting lazy AI use.
- Your mother's classroom example: students generate 3 AI essays from a prompt, then grade them, find weaknesses, rewrite. AI use is built into the assignment, making cheating debates irrelevant.
- "Raise the bar" was the right diagnosis. The folder architecture, Agent Charter, and 60/30/10 framework became the prescription — they didn't exist when this was written.

## How I'll Apply It

For my own learning: instead of treating AI as a shortcut, use it to go deeper. Claude generates the baseline; I engage with it critically — grading, rewriting, finding weaknesses. The learning is in the critique, not the generation. The bar for my own output should be above what AI can produce alone.
