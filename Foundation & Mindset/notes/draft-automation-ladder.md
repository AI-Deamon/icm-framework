# The Automation Ladder

**Source:** Skool: The Foundation / Module 2 / 2.4 The Ladder That Explains Every AI Failure

## Core Idea

There's a ladder of what you can automate, from "things the model will do for free next update" at the bottom to "things only you can decide" at the top. Companies fail when they build at the wrong rung — investing months in something that becomes a free feature when the next model drops.

## Why It Matters

- A Fortune 500 company spent $200K building a custom document processing pipeline. Microsoft Copilot added that exact feature for free three months later. Their entire moat vanished overnight because they built at the wrong layer.
- Jake's ladder (paraphrased from the transcript): basic tasks the model handles → complex workflows you can template → judgment calls only you can make → creative direction only you can set. Everything below your rung will be automated by the next update.
- The skill is not building faster — it's knowing what NOT to build. If the model will do it in 6 months, don't build it now. Build the layer above.

## How I'll Apply It

Before building any automation, ask: "Will the next Claude/GPT update make this a free feature?" If yes, stop and work on the layer above — the judgment, the taste, the client relationships, the data only I have access to.
