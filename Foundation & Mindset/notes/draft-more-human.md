# More Human: The Mount Fuji Principle

**Source:** Skool: The Archive / Module 2 / 2.3 More Human (Dec 2023, updated)

## Core Idea

AI removes the robotic, not the human. Recorded Dec 2023, Jake tells the story of climbing Mount Fuji during a typhoon and finding an injured stranger — he spent hours dragging the man up to a shrine where workers saved his life. His argument: experiences that make us human (absurd, specific, unrepeatable moments) cannot be automated by any model. AI clears space for those moments.

## Why It Matters

- The thesis validated across every sector since: Pacific Life, Colgate-Palmolive, 600+ professionals trained. AI handled the formulaic tasks (data summarization, template writing, routine analysis). Human work (judgment, context, decision-making under ambiguity) remained and became more valuable.
- "AI is highlighting what is and isn't human in us." When AI can pass a PhD review, that reveals the review process was formulaic. When AI writes a paper indistinguishable from a student's, that reveals how formulaic graduate writing has become. AI acts as a mirror for human systems.
- The scribe and printing press parallel: scribes didn't lose value — their work became specialized. The press handled scale; scribes handled meaning. Same with AI — the model handles scale, humans handle meaning.
- Updated commentary (2 years later): the "More Human" thesis extends beyond education to every industry. The Ethics Engine and governance work grew from this seed.

## How I'll Apply It

When I worry AI is making me replaceable, ask: "Is this task formulaic or uniquely human?" Formulaic work should be automated. Unique work (judgment calls, client relationships, creative direction, the Mount Fuji moments) is what I should protect and invest in. The folder structure exists to organize the human work, not the AI work.
