## **The Big Shift: From Prompting to Packaging**

A **prompt** is just an instruction.

You ask it to write an email, summarize an article, brainstorm newsletter ideas, or clean up a messy paragraph. Prompts are useful.

The trap is trying to cram *entire workflows* into ever-larger prompts—re-explaining your tone, rules, examples, and context every single time. That’s not AI adoption. That’s workflow debt in a chatbot costume.

**The real move is to stop re-prompting the same work and start packaging it.**

Prompts, skills, connectors, MCPs, hooks, scripts, and plugins are simply different layers for turning repeated effort into a reusable system.

No hype. Just structure.

---

### **1. Prompts: The One-Off Instruction**

Use a **prompt** when the task is temporary. You need help once, so you ask once and move on.

* Brainstorming titles
* Rewriting a paragraph
* Summarizing quick notes
* Drafting a message
* Explaining a concept

**Rule of thumb:** If you only need it once, use a prompt.

Prompts are like sticky notes—light, fast, and disposable. #prompts #quickwins

---

### **2. Skills: Your Repeatable Standards**

A **skill** is reusable instruction—like a recipe, checklist, or brand style guide.

It tells the AI: “When you do *this* kind of work, always follow these rules.”

Examples:

* How your team writes announcements
* Your preferred email structure
* How to summarize meeting notes
* Your specific brand voice and tone

Instead of repeating “Make it approachable, structured, warm but professional, define terms…” every time, you encode it once.

**Rule of thumb:** If you repeat the same instructions often, turn them into a skill.

This is where you stop repeating yourself and start encoding your standards. #skills #repeatablework

---

### **3. Connectors: Access to Other Systems**

A **connector** gives AI direct access to another app or service—Google Drive, Gmail, Notion, Slack, calendars, databases, CMS, etc.

Without connectors, *you* become the delivery person: copy, paste, switch tabs, copy again. You turn into the human plugin.

Connectors eliminate that friction. The AI can pull the right context instead of you shuttling data around.

**Rule of thumb:** If the AI needs information from another app, use a connector. #connectors #stopbeingtheclipboard

---

### **4. MCPs: The Universal Bridge**

**MCP** stands for **Model Context Protocol**—an open standard (introduced by Anthropic and now widely adopted) for connecting AI systems to external tools, data, and services in a consistent, secure way.

Think of it as USB-C for AI: instead of custom cables for every device, one standardized port.

MCPs make it easier and safer for AI to discover and interact with files, databases, APIs, and tools without brittle, one-off integrations.

**Rule of thumb:** When you need structured, reliable access to tools and data, MCPs provide the bridge. #mcp #structuredaccess

---

### **5. Hooks: Automatic Guardrails**

A **hook** is an automatic action that runs at a specific moment—before or after something happens.

It’s the reliable friend who says, “Did you attach the file?” or “Run the checklist first.”

Examples:

* Before publishing: check for missing fields
* After generating content: run a formatting pass
* Before sending: verify recipients and tone

Hooks catch what both humans and AI forget.

**Rule of thumb:** If something should happen automatically before or after a task, use a hook. #hooks #guardrails

---

### **6. Scripts: Exact, Deterministic Execution**

A **script** is a small program that does something precisely—no “try your best” involved.

Use scripts for:

* Validating JSON
* Checking required fields
* Testing links
* Formatting documents
* Resizing images
* Running consistency checks

Don’t ask the AI if something is probably correct. Let the computer verify it.

**Rule of thumb:** If the answer must be exact, use a script. #scripts #deterministic

---

### **7. Plugins: The Complete Packaged Workflow**

A **plugin** is the full package—bundling prompts, skills, connectors, MCP access, hooks, scripts, templates, and documentation into one reusable unit.

It’s not just an “add-on.” It’s a portable workflow that can be shared, installed, improved, and audited.

A weekly report, event publishing process, or content approval system stops being a series of manual steps and becomes a single, reliable tool.

**Rule of thumb:** If the process has multiple parts and needs to travel or scale, package it as a plugin. #plugins #packagedworkflows

---

## **The Cheat Code: Know Which Layer to Use**

**If the work is… Use this**

One-time or unique **Prompt**  
Repeated style, rules, or process **Skill**  
Needs data from another app **Connector**  
Needs standardized tool/data access **MCP**  
Needs automatic pre/post actions **Hook**  
Needs exact validation or computation **Script**  
Needs a complete, reusable workflow **Plugin**

## **Real-World Example: Publishing a Community Event**

**Step Layer Used**

Draft a friendly description from raw notes Prompt  
Always follow our event announcement style Skill  
Pull date, time, and location from calendar Connector  
Access the CMS or event database MCP / Connector  
Before publishing, verify all required info is present Hook  
Validate data fields and format Script   
Bundle the entire process for future use Plugin

You’re no longer just chatting with AI. You’re building a smart, repeatable assistant.

## **The Human Still Owns the Outcome**

Automation doesn’t replace judgment—it removes the robotic glue work.

Keep humans in charge of taste, ethics, pastoral care, community nuance, final approval, and anything that “technically works but feels off.”

**Automate the repetition. Protect the responsibility.**

## **Stop Being the Human Plugin**

The cursive note wasn’t listing jargon. It was a reminder:

Most people don’t have an AI problem. They have a **packaging** problem.

They keep doing the same work, explaining the same rules, and moving the same data—then call it “using AI.”

The better way is clearer:

* **Prompts** open the door.
* **Skills** set the standards.
* **Connectors** bring context.
* **MCPs** create reliable bridges.
* **Hooks** add guardrails.
* **Scripts** ensure truth.
* **Plugins** make it portable.

That’s the code.

Package the work. Share what works. And maybe write it down somewhere people can actually read.
