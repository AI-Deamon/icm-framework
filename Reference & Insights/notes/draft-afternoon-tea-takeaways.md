# Afternoon Tea Takeaways

**Source:** Skool: Davids Corner (community insights)

## Core Idea

The Afternoon Tea sessions are Jake's live community calls where he answers questions, shares new experiments, and builds in real-time. They're not polished lectures — they're raw thinking. The value is in watching someone who lives this methodology handle edge cases, mistakes, and live questions.

## Key Takeaways by Session

**Session 1 (Joe Fioramonti):** Scaling human judgment with AI. Joe's platform Constellation uses AI to augment human decision-making rather than replace it. The insight: AI amplifies good judgment and accelerates bad judgment — you need the human foundation first.

**Session 2 (Skills & One Client First):** Two big ideas. (1) Skills are the next evolution of context files — reusable, sharable, loadable on demand. (2) "Stop building production-ready AI. Solve for one client first." The first client teaches you what's actually needed — you don't know until someone is paying you.

**Session 5 (200 to 48,000 subscribers):** Jake's YouTube growth from 200 to 48,000 subscribers in 4 months. He attributes it to: consistent content schedule, making videos about what he's actually building (not what people say they want), and using his own methodology to produce videos faster. The growth came from showing the work, not teaching theory.

## How I'll Apply It

- Use the "one client first" rule for any new tool/process I build: solve one real case before generalizing.
- Attend or host live sessions where I work through problems in real-time — the raw process is more valuable than the polished result.
- Build in public. The subscriber growth came from showing the work, not packaging the knowledge.
