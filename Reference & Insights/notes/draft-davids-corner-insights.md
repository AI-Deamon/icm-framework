# Davids Corner: Community Insights

**Source:** Skool: Davids Corner (curated member posts)

## Core Idea

Davids Corner is the bridge between Jake's formal course material and what the community is actually building. These are curated posts from members — real people applying ICM in production — aggregated by David to save everyone else from scrolling through 50,000 messages.

## Key Insights from the Posts

- **Stop Prompting. Start Defining Outcomes.** "A prompt is a wish. A brief is a contract." Treat AI like a contractor — define the outcome, not just the action. The quality of output is capped by the clarity of input.
- **Flip the Script (Value-Based Pricing).** The old model (hourly consulting, custom agents, prompt engineering) has hit its limit. Sell measurable value: lead with outcomes, structure around shared success metrics, price based on impact.
- **Curtis Hays on Infrastructure.** 13 years in paid media + 13 years building IT systems. His thesis: "AI is not magic and it's not just infrastructure. It is a system for protecting and scaling judgment." The uniqueness of WHY you're building is the only thing that can't be replicated.
- **Four Phases Before Building.** (Ari's methodology) Brainstorm → Plan → Hand-off Doc → Dispatch. "The build is the easy part. The thinking is the work." If you skip structured planning, you spend 90% of your time babysitting outputs.
- **Vibe Coding Rules.** Community member Noah's `vibe-code-rules.md` — a lightweight, repeatable set of guardrails that treats the filesystem as the architecture. Same principle as AGENTS.md, distilled for rapid prototyping.
- **Obsidian is Bloat.** When running agentic workflows, the folder system IS the architecture and the UI. Obsidian is a redundant layer. VS Code + plain text is leaner and more portable.

## How I'll Apply It

Davids Corner is my reminder that the system is meant to be used, not perfected. The most actionable insight: stop treating prompts as one-off wishes and start writing briefs as contracts. Move from "make me X" to "here's the outcome, here's what success looks like."
