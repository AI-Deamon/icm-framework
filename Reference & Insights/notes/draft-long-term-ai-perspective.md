# Long-Term AI Perspective (Interview with Matt, NLP Logics)

**Source:** Skool: The Archive / Module 1 (retrospective framing)

## Core Idea

Matt started an AI company in 2011 — well over a decade before the hype. His perspective: the companies that survive AI waves are the ones that focus on data moats and domain expertise, not model capabilities. Models are a commodity that everyone gets access to; your data and your understanding of the problem is what differentiates you.

## Why It Matters

- 2011 AI was completely different — no deep learning boom, no transformers, no LLMs. Matt's company NLP Logics survived by focusing on natural language processing for enterprise clients who needed domain-specific solutions that general models couldn't handle.
- His take on the next decade: the models become infrastructure (like electricity), the value moves to data ownership, domain-specific training, and human-in-the-loop systems that capture institutional knowledge.
- Jake's interpretation (which he states explicitly): this confirms the folder workspace thesis. Models are disposable; your context files (which encode your domain knowledge) are the lasting asset.

## How I'll Apply It

- Prioritize building context files (domain knowledge) over learning specific model APIs.
- When the next model drops, don't ask "what can it do?" — ask "what data/context do I have that it can't get anywhere else?"
- Focus on a specific domain where I have unique understanding — that's the moat.
