# Knowing What Not to Automate

**Source:** Skool: The Foundation / Module 2 / 2.4 (The Ladder) extension

## Core Idea

The real skill in the AI era is not building faster — it's developing the judgment to know what should stay manual. Jake presents this as a slide-based deep dive: every automation decision is a trade-off between efficiency (doing it faster) and understanding (doing it yourself teaches you something).

## Why It Matters

- Automating a task you don't understand is dangerous — you lose the feedback loop that teaches you whether the output is good. If you automate data analysis before you know how to read a dataset, you'll confidently present wrong answers.
- The prompt library Jake references isn't copy-paste prompts — they're designed around this idea. Each prompt is a template that forces you to engage with the output, not just accept it.
- He puts a condensed prompt at the bottom of every slide. His insight: the slide itself becomes the prompt. You can screenshot it and feed it to the AI — the visual layout IS the instruction structure.

## How I'll Apply It

Before automating anything, ask: "Will automating this make me better or worse at judging the quality of the output?" If automating breaks my feedback loop, I should do it manually until I develop taste. Then automate the execution, never the judgment.
